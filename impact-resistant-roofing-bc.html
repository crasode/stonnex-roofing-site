Why It Matters in 2026

BC's Weather Is Changing.
Your Roof Should Reflect That.

Environment Canada data shows an increase in high-intensity precipitation events, wind storms, and hail events across BC's Lower Mainland and Fraser Valley over the past decade. Communities in Abbotsford, Langley, Chilliwack, and the Fraser Valley corridor have experienced documented hail damage. North Shore properties are exposed to wind-driven debris from the mountain corridors. Delta and Richmond face elevated wind loading from open terrain.

Standard architectural shingles are designed for normal weather loads. Class 4 impact-resistant shingles are engineered for the conditions BC is increasingly experiencing. The cost premium over standard shingles is modest. The protection difference is significant.

Class 4 Rating Explained

UL 2218 Class 4 is the highest impact classification for roofing. The shingle must survive a 2-inch steel ball dropped from 20 feet with no crack or puncture. Standard shingles carry no impact rating or Class 1-2 ratings that do not qualify for insurance discounts.

Insurance Premium Reduction

Many major BC home insurers — including Intact, Wawanesa, Aviva, and others — offer documented premium reductions of 5-30% for Class 4 installations. The reduction varies by insurer and policy. Stonnex provides certification documents needed to file the discount claim with your insurer.

Extended Shingle Lifespan

Impact-resistant shingles use a reinforced fiberglass mat and polymer-modified asphalt that resists granule loss, bruising, and cracking from normal weather wear — not just extreme events. This contributes to a longer effective lifespan compared to standard architectural shingles.

Wind Resistance Upgrade

Class 4 shingles are typically paired with enhanced wind ratings of 130-160 km/h. Most carry a Class H wind rating, versus the 110-130 km/h typical of standard architectural shingles. Critical for exposed Fraser Valley and North Shore properties.

Common Questions

Answered.

What are impact-resistant shingles?
Impact-resistant shingles are engineered asphalt shingles rated to withstand hail, falling debris, and high-wind events beyond what standard shingles can handle. The top classification is Class 4, which means the shingle has passed a steel ball drop test simulating a 2-inch hailstone at terminal velocity. They feature a reinforced fiberglass mat and modified asphalt formulation that resists cracking and bruising on impact.
Do impact-resistant shingles reduce home insurance premiums in BC?
Many BC home insurance providers offer premium discounts for Class 4 impact-resistant shingles, as they significantly reduce the probability of storm-related roof damage claims. The discount varies by insurer and policy, but is commonly in the range of 5-30% on the roof-related portion of your premium. Stonnex recommends contacting your insurer before installation to confirm your specific discount eligibility.
Are impact-resistant shingles worth it in BC?
Yes, particularly in BC communities with documented storm and wind exposure including Abbotsford, Langley, Chilliwack, Delta, and exposed North Shore properties. The combination of a longer shingle lifespan, reduced maintenance cycle, and potential insurance premium reduction typically offsets the modest upfront premium over a standard architectural shingle installation within 5-10 years.
What is a Class 4 impact rating?
Class 4 is the highest impact-resistance classification under UL 2218, the North American testing standard for impact-resistant roofing. It requires the shingle to pass a steel ball drop test simulating a 2-inch steel sphere at terminal velocity with no cracking or puncture. Class 4 shingles are the product specified for insurance discounts and for properties in high storm-exposure zones.
