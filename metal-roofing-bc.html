Metal Roofing BC

Why BC Homeowners Are
Choosing Metal in 2026

Metal roofing has been the standard for commercial and industrial buildings in BC for decades. In 2026, residential adoption is accelerating — driven by BC's increasingly volatile weather, the rising cost of repeat asphalt replacements, and the availability of premium aesthetics including matte black standing seam and warm neutral metal tile profiles that suit West Coast residential architecture.

A quality asphalt roof in BC lasts 20-25 years. A quality metal roof lasts 40-50 years. For a homeowner who plans to remain in their property, that represents one full replacement cycle eliminated — often offsetting the higher upfront cost entirely when lifecycle cost is assessed properly.

Best for Longevity

Standing Seam Metal

The premium residential metal system. Concealed fasteners eliminate penetration-point failures. Raised seams provide superior drainage in BC's high-rainfall climate. Available in steel, aluminum, and Galvalume substrates.

Lifespan: 50+ years  |  Best for: Executive, character, and coastal homes

Best Aesthetic Range

Metal Tile & Shake

Designed to replicate the profile of cedar shake or clay tile with the durability of steel. Popular on North Shore and Fraser Valley homes where heritage aesthetic matters. Lighter than tile, stronger than wood.

Lifespan: 40-50 years  |  Best for: Character homes, North Shore, Langley acreage

Most Cost-Effective Entry

Corrugated & R-Panel Steel

Exposed-fastener systems offer metal durability at a lower installed cost. Suited to secondary structures, agricultural buildings, and budget-conscious homeowners who want metal performance without standing seam pricing.

Lifespan: 25-40 years  |  Best for: Outbuildings, detached garages, budget builds

Common Questions

Metal Roofing BC
Answered.

How much does metal roofing cost in BC?
Metal roofing in BC typically ranges from 8,000 to 5,000 for a standard residential home depending on the system type (standing seam vs. metal tile vs. exposed fastener), roof complexity, and size. While the upfront cost is higher than asphalt, the 40-50 year lifespan and minimal maintenance cost means total cost of ownership is often lower over time. Stonnex provides written fixed-price quotes after a complimentary on-site inspection.
What is the best metal roofing system for BC rain?
Standing seam metal roofing is the preferred system for BC's high-rainfall climate. The concealed fastener design eliminates the penetration points that cause leaks in exposed-fastener systems. The raised seams also provide excellent drainage performance in BC's heavy rain events. Stonnex recommends Galvalume steel or aluminum substrates for BC's coastal and near-coastal properties.
How long does metal roofing last in BC?
Quality metal roofing systems installed by certified contractors carry a 40-50 year expected lifespan under BC's climate conditions. Standing seam systems with quality Galvalume or painted steel substrates often outlast the warranty period. Compared to 20-25 year asphalt shingles, this represents a full roof replacement cycle saved over the lifespan of the property.
Does Stonnex install standing seam metal roofing?
Yes. Stonnex installs standing seam, metal tile, and steel panel metal roofing systems across Vancouver, the North Shore, Burnaby, Surrey, Langley, and all Lower Mainland communities. We provide free on-site consultations to assess your home's suitability and recommend the most appropriate system.
Is metal roofing good for snow in North Vancouver?
Yes. Metal roofing is well-suited to North Vancouver's snow conditions. The smooth surface allows snow to shed naturally, reducing structural load. Standing seam systems with appropriate pitch handle heavy snowfall effectively. Snow guards can be installed at eaves to control avalanche shedding where required.
