Why It Matters

The Real Cost of
Waiting

A failing roof doesn't stay contained. The longer it sits, the more it costs — and not just to fix the roof.

01
Small Leaks Become Structural Damage
A minor leak through one BC winter can saturate insulation, rot roof decking, and compromise structural framing. A $3,000 repair becomes a $15,000 rebuild.
02
Mould Remediation Adds Thousands More
Persistent moisture creates mould within 24–48 hours. Professional remediation in BC averages $5,000–$15,000 — on top of whatever roofing costs you were already avoiding.
03
Emergency Repairs Cost 3× More
A planned replacement is efficient and competitively priced. An emergency repair after a storm commands premium rates, rushed timelines, and limited contractor availability.
04
A Failing Roof Kills Your Resale
Buyers and home inspectors flag roof condition immediately. An aging roof either kills deals or forces price reductions averaging $10,000+ — far more than a timely replacement.
05
Energy Costs Rise Year Over Year
Failing insulation and compromised ventilation drive up heating and cooling bills every season. A properly installed roof system pays for itself over time in reduced energy costs.
06
Your Warranty May Already Be Void
Most manufacturer warranties are voided by deferred maintenance. If a previous contractor skipped proper installation steps, you may have no warranty protection at all right now.
