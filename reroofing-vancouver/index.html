Is It Time?

Signs Your Vancouver Home
Needs a New Roof

Roof Age

Most asphalt shingle roofs in Metro Vancouver last 20–30 years. If yours is approaching or past that age, plan for replacement before leaks start.

Frequent Repairs

Calling a roofer every year or patching the same areas repeatedly means cumulative repair costs are exceeding the value — replacement makes more sense.

Granule Loss

Heavy granule buildup in your gutters means your shingles are losing their UV protection. Vancouver's 1,150mm of annual rain will accelerate deterioration quickly from this point.

Curling or Cracking

Shingles that are curling at the edges or showing visible cracking can no longer shed water properly. This is an immediate replacement indicator.

Common Questions

Reroofing Vancouver
FAQs

How long does reroofing take in Vancouver?
Most Vancouver residential reroofs are completed in one to two days. Larger or more complex homes may take three days. We confirm the schedule before your project starts.
How much does reroofing cost in Vancouver?
Cost depends on roof size, pitch, material selection, and deck condition. Most residential reroofs in the Metro Vancouver range from $8,000 to $25,000. We provide free written quotes — book an inspection for an accurate number for your home.
Do you always do a full tear-off?
Yes. We always remove the existing roofing system before installing new material. This allows us to inspect the decking, address moisture damage, and ensure your new roof achieves its full lifespan — especially important given Vancouver's 1,150mm of annual rainfall.
Are you licensed to work in Vancouver?
Yes. Stonnex is a licensed BC contractor, WorkSafeBC registered, and carries $5M liability insurance. We work throughout Metro Vancouver and the Lower Mainland.
What warranty do I get on my Vancouver reroof?
Every project includes a written workmanship warranty from Stonnex plus the manufacturer's material warranty, ranging from 25 to 50 years depending on the product selected.
